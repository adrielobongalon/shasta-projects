Cars - Carbon Dioxide

When it comes to air pollution, the first thing you'd probably think of is cars. Known for producing a multitude of greenhouse gases, especially carbon dioxide (CO2), cars are responsible a significant fraction of all CO2 emissions.

Assumptions and Limitations

The information above is nice, but it's important to keep in mind that it assumes several things. First off, it assumes that the miles driven over 4 years is accurate. The actual figure is slightly different, as the milage on the car dashboard also included a relatively small number of miles used to transport the car to the dealership and for test drives by customers before my family bought the car. Additionally, the car was not owned for exactly 4 years. However, it is close enough to the actual value to make a rough estimate. Further, the 119 g CO2 / km figure was synthesised from Contract Hire and Leasing, Next Green Car, and Fleet News, all of which are UK-based agencies. Given that my family's car is the American variant of the Mazda3, the actual carbon dioxide output may be slightly different, especially since the EU has different environtmental regulations than the US. There were no reliable sources I could find that gave the US version's CO2 output, so I had to find the closest UK equivalent I could. Lastly, the masses for a mole of carbon and oxygen, as well as the numerical equivalent of a mole, are rounded, meaning that my results are not 100% precise.
