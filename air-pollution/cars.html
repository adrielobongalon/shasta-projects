Cars - Carbon Dioxide

When it comes to air pollution, the first thing you'd probably think of is cars. Known for producing a multitude of greenhouse gases, especially carbon dioxide (C02), cars are responsible a significant fraction of all C02 emissions.

Just as a reminder, my research question for this topic was:
"On average, how many carbon dioxide molecules does my familyâ€™s car, a 2013 Mazda 3, emit annually?"

This is what the car looks like.

So here's some of the data that I gathered specifically from the dashboard and the manuals on the car.

  • make and model: 2013 Mazda 3 hatchback
  • overall mpg rating: 32 miles per gallon (mpg)
  • engine capacity: 2.0 litres
  • fuel type: petrol
  • mileage: 60,193 miles
  • owned since: 2013 (~4 years)

And here's the data I gathered online.

  • CO2 emissions: 119 g/km

Note that not all of this data was necessary in the calculation, but was necessary for the research. There are several variants of the Mazda 3, and finding the right one was not easy.

And from there, I just made the following calculations:

$$ \begin{equation} \label{eq1} \begin{split} molar \hspace{1.5mm} mass \hspace{1.5mm} of \hspace{1.5mm} CO_2 & = molar \hspace{1.5mm} mass \hspace{1.5mm} of \hspace{1.5mm} carbon + ( 2 \times molar \hspace{1.5mm} mass \hspace{1.5mm} of \hspace{1.5mm} oxygen ) \\ & = 12.0107 \hspace{1.5mm} g \hspace{1.5mm} C / mol \hspace{1.5mm} C + ( 2 \times 15.9994 \hspace{1.5mm} g \hspace{1.5mm} O / mol \hspace{1.5mm} O ) \\ & = 44.0095 \hspace{1.5mm} g \hspace{1.5mm} CO_2 / mol \hspace{1.5mm} CO_2 \end{split} \end{equation} $$

$$ ({{60193 \hspace{1.5mm} mi} \over {4 \hspace{1.5mm} yrs}}) \times ({{1.609344 \hspace{1.5mm} km} \over {1 \hspace{1.5mm} mi}}) \times ({{119 \hspace{1.5mm} g \hspace{1.5mm} CO_2} \over {1 \hspace{1.5mm} km}}) \times ({{1 \hspace{1.5mm} mol \hspace{1.5mm} CO_2} \over {44.0095 \hspace{1.5mm} g \hspace{1.5mm} CO_2}}) \times ({{6.022140857 \times 10^{23} \hspace{1.5mm} molecules \hspace{1.5mm} CO_2} \over {1 \hspace{1.5mm} mol \hspace{1.5mm} CO_2}}) \\ = 3.9435 \times 10^{28} \hspace{1.5mm} molecules \hspace{1.5mm} CO_2/year $$
